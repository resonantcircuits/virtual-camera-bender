How Virtual Camera Bender Works

The Signal Path

Camera preset → Macro dials → Circuit modules → Image.

Cameras are starting points, not limits. Clicking one copies its full settings into the editor. From there everything is yours: every slider, randomizer, and module works from the current state, no matter which camera you started with.

Macros vs Advanced Circuit

Macro dials are the big knobs. Moving one re-tunes the module parameters underneath it — so they overwrite fine tweaks in that area. Use them to rough in a look.

Advanced Circuit is the per-module truth: every parameter of every effect. Use it to fine-tune after the macros get you close. Each module header has three buttons: R re-rolls just that module (keeps the seed, so everything else renders identically), S solos it so you see only its effect, and the lamp switches it on or off.

Randomize

Randomize always bends the current settings — it never resets to a preset.

Mode sets how hard it hits: Bent is gentle, Damaged is clearly broken, Shorted is violent, Explore draws from the full range. The per-module R dice use this mode too.

Global builds a whole new camera (new name, new seed, fresh macros, and a fresh pick of which modules are active). The family buttons (Color, Melt, Burn, Noise, Cheap, Memory) re-roll only that damage domain and leave the rest alone — good for keeping a look you like while re-rolling the part you don't.

Seed

The seed steers every random pattern in the render: noise grain, drip positions, block glitches. Reroll keeps all your settings but redraws the damage in new places. Same settings + same seed = same image.

Comparing

Split A/B overlays the original left of a divider you drag across the image. Hold · Original (or holding C) flashes the untouched photo. Export always renders at full source resolution.

Shortcuts
